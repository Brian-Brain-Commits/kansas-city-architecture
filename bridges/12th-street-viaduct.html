The Descent to the Basin

The 12th Street Viaduct is the city's signature approach to the West Bottoms: an elevated concrete roadway that leaves downtown at Quality Hill's western edge, curves south-and-west in a shallow S-shape, and descends roughly 80 feet over a half-mile to the basin floor. Drivers crossing it experience a uniquely Kansas City sequence — the downtown skyline receding behind, the limestone bluff falling away to the right, the Bottoms opening below as warehouses, rail yards, and the glittering band of the Kansas River come into view. Few American cities have an entrance to a neighborhood that reads as dramatically as a geographic act.

The current viaduct is a 2002 rebuild of the original 1915 structure. The roadway alignment follows the historic path and the concrete-deck profile carries the same 1915 geometry, but the substructure and railings are modern. Pedestrian access is maintained via a separate sidewalk. The Hereford bull statue, installed 1953 on the earlier viaduct, was relocated back to its plinth after the 2002 reconstruction.

The First-Weekend Warehouses

The West Bottoms antique district is one of the country's largest concentrations of antique, vintage, and salvage retail, operating out of roughly a dozen surviving late-Victorian warehouse buildings along Mulberry, St. Louis, and the cross streets between 8th and 13th. Most stores open only on the first weekend of each month — a rhythm set by logistics (moving antique inventory into a multi-story warehouse needs dedicated days) and maintained by tradition. On First Weekends, the district draws tens of thousands of visitors; the rest of the month it is mostly quiet, which is part of its character.

What makes the antique district architecturally compelling is the same thing that makes the haunted district work: the buildings are not themed. The pressed-brick facades, cast-iron storefront columns, timber-frame interior structure, freight elevators, and wrought-iron fire escapes are original to the 1880s–1910s industrial construction. Vendors operate inside the preserved bones. A Saturday walk through the district in January, with snow on the brick and a pot of coffee at the cash register, feels like a time that never quite was anywhere else.

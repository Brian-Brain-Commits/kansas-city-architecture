Kansas City has a long history of buildings that vanished: Convention Hall burned the night before the 1900 Democratic National Convention and was rebuilt in ninety days; the original Union Depot in the West Bottoms was abandoned when the new Union Station opened in 1914; entire blocks of the East Side were cleared in the urban renewal demolitions of the 1950s and 60s. This page joins them in the archive of what was.

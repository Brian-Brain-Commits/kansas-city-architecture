The
Map

Everything we've covered, in one place.

This map is every building, fountain, neighborhood, and historic site covered on Look Up, Kansas City, plotted on a single schematic of the city. It's not geographically precise — it's a reference, not a navigation aid. Use it to see the shape of what this site is about, or to find your way to a specific place.

What You're Looking At

This is a schematic map, not a geographic one. The buildings are in roughly the right places relative to each other, but the distances aren't to scale and the outlines aren't precise. If you want to actually get from Liberty Memorial to the Plaza, use a real map. If you want to understand how Look Up, Kansas City sees the city — what we've written about, what we've left out, how the pieces relate to each other — this is the overview.

The map is biased toward the places the site covers. The Northland (everything north of the river) is essentially absent, as are the eastern and southern suburbs. Quality Hill, Hyde Park, Volker, Waldo, and the whole stretch of the East Side beyond 18th & Vine are under-represented. Those are gaps worth closing; they are the work the site still has to do.

What's Not Here

The map is missing a lot. The Quality Hill mansions — the first wealthy residential neighborhood in Kansas City, now partially preserved — don't have their own page yet. Neither do Hyde Park, Waldo, or Volker. The whole stretch of the East Side beyond 18th & Vine is under-represented. The Northland, everything north of the Missouri River, is essentially not on the site at all. None of that is because those places don't matter. It's because the site is a work in progress, and these are the next pages to write.

If you find gaps in the coverage or mistakes in the placement of markers, please let us know. This map will get better as the site does.

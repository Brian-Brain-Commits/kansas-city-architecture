Look Up, Kansas City

Further
Reading

Books and resources for going deeper. Organized by subject — architecture, history, jazz, and the city's built environment.

◆

Architecture & Urban Form

Sherry Lamb Schirmer 2002

A City Divided: The Racial Landscape of Kansas City, 1900–1960

The essential account of how race and real estate shaped Kansas City's built environment across the first half of the twentieth century — covering the Nichols covenants, redlining, and their physical legacy in the city's geography.

University of Missouri Press
William S. Worley 1990

J.C. Nichols and the Shaping of Kansas City

The most comprehensive biography of Nichols and the Country Club District — thorough on the real estate mechanics, the boulevard strategy, and the architectural guidelines that produced Brookside, Mission Hills, and the Plaza.

University of Missouri Press
Jane Flynn 2000

Kansas City Women of Independent Minds

Profiles of women who shaped the city's cultural institutions — useful for understanding the civic organizations that drove preservation and the arts philanthropy behind many of the fountains and parks.

Fifield Publishing
Landmarks Commission of Kansas City Various

Kansas City Landmarks Survey Reports

The official survey documentation for Kansas City's historic buildings — dry reading, but authoritative on construction dates, architects, and building histories. Available through the city planning department and Kansas City Public Library special collections.

City of Kansas City
◆

History & Politics

Lawrence H. Larsen & Nancy J. Hulston 1997

Pendergast!

The standard biography of Tom Pendergast — accessible and well-researched, covering the machine's mechanics, its relationship to the construction boom, and its eventual collapse under federal prosecution.

University of Missouri Press
A. Theodore Brown 1963

The Politics of Reform: Kansas City's Municipal Government, 1925–1950

Academic but readable — the best account of how the Ten-Year Plan was conceived, funded, and executed, and the political forces that made it possible under Pendergast's machine.

Community Studies
Alonzo L. Hamby 1995

Man of the People: A Life of Harry S. Truman

The most thorough Truman biography — essential for understanding how Kansas City's political culture shaped the man and the president, and what Truman's rise says about machine politics and its limits.

Oxford University Press
Diane Mutti Burke 2010

On Slavery's Border: Missouri's Small-Slaveholding Households, 1815–1865

Essential context for the Westport and Civil War history — explains why Missouri's border position made Kansas City's Civil War experience so much more complicated than that of states with clearer allegiances.

University of Georgia Press
◆

Jazz, Music & the 18th & Vine District

Frank Driggs & Chuck Haddix 2005

Kansas City Jazz: From Ragtime to Bebop — A History

The definitive history of KC jazz — covers the specific geographic and cultural conditions of 18th and Vine, the role of the Pendergast years in the scene's development, and the musicians who came out of it.

Oxford University Press
Gary Giddins 1987

Celebrating Bird: The Triumph of Charlie Parker

Short, beautifully written — the best introduction to Charlie Parker and to what Kansas City's musical environment produced. Essential for visitors to the American Jazz Museum who want context before they go.

Beech Tree Books
Ross Russell 1973

Bird Lives! The High Life and Hard Times of Charlie (Yardbird) Parker

Vivid and detailed — Ross Russell knew Parker and the KC scene firsthand. The portrait of the 18th and Vine district in the 1930s and early 1940s is irreplaceable, however colored by Russell's perspective.

Charterhouse
◆

Rivers, Geography & the Natural City

John Madson 1985

Up on the River: An Upper Mississippi Chronicle

Not specifically about Kansas City, but the best prose account of what the Missouri-Mississippi river system is — essential context for understanding why Kansas City's location made it inevitable.

Schocken Books
William Least Heat-Moon 1991

PrairyErth: A Deep Map

A close reading of Chase County, Kansas — directly relevant to understanding the cultural and geographic world from which Kansas City emerged. One of the great American books about place and landscape.

Houghton Mifflin

Local Resources & Archives

Kansas City Public Library Ongoing

Missouri Valley Special Collections

The largest repository of Kansas City history — photographs, maps, architectural drawings, and newspaper archives. The digital collections are accessible online; the physical collections require an appointment but are open to the public.

kclibrary.org/special-collections
National WWI Museum Ongoing

National WWI Museum & Memorial Library and Archives

The research library at the Liberty Memorial — the finest WWI research collection in the Western Hemisphere. Open to researchers by appointment; the online finding aids are comprehensive.

theworldwar.org
Historic Kansas City Foundation Ongoing

Historic Kansas City Foundation Resources

Advocacy and documentation organization for KC historic preservation — publishes reports on endangered buildings and maintains records of preservation projects. A good resource for understanding what is at risk and what has been saved.

historickansascity.org

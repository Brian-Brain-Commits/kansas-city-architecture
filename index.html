Why This Site

Kansas City built a skyline in one decade and then mostly forgot to brag about it. The Art Deco towers downtown, the Spanish Colonial discipline of the Plaza, the Beaux-Arts grandeur of Union Station, the boulevard-and-fountain system that connects them — this is one of the most coherent and underappreciated architectural environments in the United States, and it sits in a city that does not announce itself.

This site is for the kind of visitor or resident who wants to understand what they're looking at. It is not a tourism brochure and it is not a Wikipedia summary. It tries to do close looking, to ground each building in the politics and economics that produced it, and to be honest about what was complicated — the J.C. Nichols legacy of restrictive deed covenants, the urban renewal demolitions of the mid-century, the buildings the city tore down before deciding which were worth saving.

Use the walking tours if you have a free afternoon. Use the history chapters if you want to understand how the city built itself. Use the building pages as a reference. The site rewards browsing.

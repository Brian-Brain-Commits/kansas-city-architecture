Kansas City, Missouri — 1900s–1940s — The City That Built to Last

The Half-Century That Shaped the Skyline

The buildings on this page were constructed between the 1910s and the late 1930s, a period when Kansas City invested in its physical identity with a confidence that bordered on the reckless. The Ten-Year Plan of 1926 — a $40 million bond program for civic construction — was the formal expression of that confidence, but the private sector had already been building for a decade before the city formally organized around it. Banks, utilities, theaters, and transportation companies all competed to claim the skyline.

What resulted is a downtown core of exceptional architectural coherence: a cluster of Art Deco and Beaux-Arts buildings in limestone, granite, and terracotta whose quality has not dimmed. These structures are not museum pieces — they remain in active use, as seats of government, concert halls, transit hubs, and commercial towers. They are Kansas City's most durable civic achievement.

The Buildings

Union Station

1914 / Restored 1999 • Beaux-Arts

A Beaux-Arts colossus anchoring the southern approach to downtown — once the third-largest train station in America, now a celebrated cultural destination.

Midland Theatre

1927 • French Baroque • 3,600 Seats

An opulent atmospheric theater whose gilded interior transports audiences to a vanished golden age of entertainment and civic pride.

Kansas City Power & Light Building

1931 • Art Deco • 476 ft

A thirty-six-story tower of limestone and bronze, its crown illuminated nightly — the defining beacon of the KC skyline for half a century.

Fidelity Bank Building

1931 • Art Deco • 454 ft

Distinguished by its ornate terracotta facade and dramatically setback upper floors — a textbook masterwork of Deco massing.

Liberty Memorial

1926 • Egyptian Revival • 217 ft

A 217-foot Egyptian Revival tower — America's national WWI memorial — dedicated by all five Allied supreme commanders in a ceremony that drew 100,000 people.

Municipal Auditorium

1935 • PWA Moderne • 2,600 Seats

One of the finest civic Art Deco buildings in the nation, born from the city's bold Ten-Year Plan and still hosting major events.

City Hall

1937 • Art Deco • 443 ft

Thirty floors of Indiana limestone rise in a bold stepped silhouette — the seat of city government and the capstone of the Ten-Year Plan.

Bryant Building

1931 • Art Deco • 205 ft

The Deco building that isn't famous — a quiet Art Deco office tower at 11th and Grand that has held its corner for nearly a century.

Soldiers & Sailors Memorial

1905 • Granite Obelisk

Kansas City's older war memorial — a granite obelisk honoring Union Civil War veterans, relocated in 1959 to Budd Park along the Kessler boulevard system.

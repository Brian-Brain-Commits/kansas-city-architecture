The Exposed-Structure Arena

Completed in 1974 on what had been Stockyards land, Kemper Arena was Helmut Jahn's breakthrough project and one of the first major American arenas to expose its structural frame as the building's primary aesthetic. Three enormous space-frame trusses spanned the roof, with the support columns and bracing left visible rather than clad — an architectural statement that a building's structure could carry its own expression without decorative finish.

The arena was named for R. Crosby Kemper Jr., the Kansas City banker and civic figure who championed the project. It hosted the 1976 Republican National Convention, Big 12 basketball tournaments for years, countless concerts, the American Royal livestock show each November (the historical echo of the site's Stockyards past), and — most memorably for the building's story — a partial roof collapse in 1979 that led to structural revisions.

From Kemper to Mosaic

The arena was renamed Mosaic Arena in 2023 under a naming-rights agreement with Mosaic Life Care. For many Kansas Citians, the stencilled KEMPER signage visible in older photography (and on the West Bottoms scene above) is still the name of the building — a pattern familiar to anyone who has lived through a corporate rename.

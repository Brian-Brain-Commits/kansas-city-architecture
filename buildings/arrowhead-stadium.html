The Loudest Stadium in the World

Arrowhead Stadium opened on August 12, 1972, as part of the Truman Sports Complex — a twin-stadium campus built simultaneously with Kauffman Stadium on 480 acres east of downtown. From its first season it was recognized as something architecturally and acoustically distinct: a purpose-built football venue whose steep, cantilevered seating bowl concentrates crowd noise in a way that few structures in sports have matched before or since.

In 2014, Arrowhead set the Guinness World Record for loudest outdoor stadium at 142.2 decibels — a mark that is a function not just of Chiefs fans but of the building's geometry. The bowl narrows toward the field in a compressed ellipse, the upper decks lean in rather than lean back, and the roof overhang reflects sound downward. The architecture produces the sound; the fans fill it.

◆

Kivett & Myers and the Truman Sports Complex

The Truman Sports Complex was designed by the Kansas City firm Kivett & Myers, one of the most prolific civic architecture practices in the city's history. The commission presented an unusual design problem: two major sports venues of entirely different geometries — an oval baseball park and an elongated football stadium — sharing a campus and a civic identity. The solution was to treat them as a matched pair: similar in scale and material palette, different in form, united by shared infrastructure and approach roads.

The result is one of the few sports campuses in America where both major tenants share a purpose-built home. Most cities with NFL and MLB teams have one downtown venue and one suburban facility built decades apart. Kansas City built both at once, on the same land, with the same architectural intention — an act of civic planning that has made the complex a model studied by other cities ever since.

The 2007 Renovation

In 2007 the stadium underwent a comprehensive renovation costing $375 million — the largest capital improvement in the facility's history. New club levels, premium seating, and expanded concourses were added without altering the essential geometry of the bowl. The renovation was notable for preserving the acoustic properties that made Arrowhead famous: the architects studied the original bowl carefully and rebuilt around it rather than over it.

The renovated stadium hosted Super Bowl LIV in 2020, won by the Kansas City Chiefs — the first Super Bowl played at Arrowhead, and the city's first NFL championship since 1970. It is scheduled to host the 2026 FIFA World Cup alongside stadiums in fifteen other American, Canadian, and Mexican cities, marking the first time Arrowhead will be used for international football.

The Campus That Named a City

The Truman Sports Complex sits six miles east of downtown on US-50, in a location that was deliberately suburban — accessible by interstate from the growing ring of Johnson County suburbs that was shifting Kansas City's population gravity in the early 1970s. That siting decision has defined the sports geography of the metropolitan area ever since.

Kansas City without Arrowhead is difficult to imagine. The Chiefs, who arrived from Dallas in 1963 thanks to the personal guarantees of Mayor H. Roe Bartle, have played here for more than fifty years. The stadium that was built for them has become one of the fixed landmarks of the city's identity — as recognizable as City Hall or the Union Station headhouse, and as deeply identified with what Kansas City means to the people who live here.

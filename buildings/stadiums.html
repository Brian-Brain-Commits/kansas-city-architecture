Kansas City, Missouri — 1972–2024 — A City That Builds for Sport

Three Stadiums, Three Eras

Kansas City's stadium story spans more than fifty years and three distinct chapters. In 1972 and 1973, the city built the Truman Sports Complex — a twin-stadium campus for the Chiefs and the Royals that remains one of the most ambitious sports construction projects in American history. In 2007, the city bet $276 million on T-Mobile Center — an arena built without a confirmed anchor tenant, on civic faith alone. And in 2024, a women's soccer team built the first stadium in the world purpose-designed for a professional women's sports club, privately financed, on the Missouri River waterfront.

Taken together, these buildings do something that purely civic or commercial architecture cannot: they mark the moments when Kansas City decided what kind of sports city it wanted to be. Each one is an argument about the city's future made in concrete, steel, and glass.

The loudest outdoor stadium on record — a purpose-built NFL venue whose compressed bowl and cantilevered decks transform crowd noise into architecture.

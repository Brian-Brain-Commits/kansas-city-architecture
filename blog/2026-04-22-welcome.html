Welcome to the Notebook

What the blog is, what it isn't, and why it lives separate from the reference site.

This is the first post of what I'm calling the notebook. Look Up, Kansas City has been a reference site: history pages, building pages, neighborhood profiles, the work of laying out a city in public. The reference pages are written carefully, with the voice of a guide trying to explain a city to somebody who wants to understand it. That voice is mostly impersonal. The "I" doesn't show up much.

The blog is where I can write differently. Shorter or longer, quieter or more argumentative, with the "I" back where it belongs when the work is observational. It's a place for specific walks, particular buildings I've been watching, things I'm reading, arguments I'm having with the city about what it is and what it could be. The encyclopedia does one job; the notebook does another.

What will go here: reactions to news about a specific restoration, a demolition, an announcement. Photo essays from afternoons walking with a camera. Reading notes from the books that keep teaching me about this place. Process pieces about how the reference site gets made. Small observations from crossing a street at an unexpected hour. The voice will be less formal than the reference pages. Nothing here is authoritative in the way a reference page tries to be.

Posts will appear when they appear. Sometimes multiple in a week; sometimes nothing for a month. The archive builds slowly. If you have something to add, correct, or argue with — I'd like to hear from you.

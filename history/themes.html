Kansas City History

Themes
The Arguments the City Has With Itself

Some stories cut across eras. The Missouri River that made the city possible, then receded from civic attention. The streetcar system that shaped the geography of the metro and reinforced its racial boundaries. The buildings demolished in waves of urban renewal. The jazz tradition that emerged from segregation and is now one of the city's signature exports. These are the recurring arguments that Kansas City has with itself.

Kansas City on Foot

Kansas City was built to be seen from a car — the boulevard system, the parkways, the Plaza's Spanish towers rising above the treetops on the drive south. But the city's architecture reveals itself differently on foot. The Art Deco ornament is at eye level and above: you have to stop, look up, and let your eyes trace the terra cotta from the street cornice to the setback tower. The fountain program works the same way — you find it by walking, turning a corner, discovering a bronze figure catching afternoon light in a niche you'd never notice from a car.

These four tours are designed for visitors who want to see Kansas City the way its architects intended it to be seen. Each is self-guided, walkable in under two hours, and built around a coherent theme — a style, a district, a corridor of connected buildings. Print them out before you go, or read them on your phone. The city will do the rest.

Downtown • 1.2 miles

The Art Deco Mile

Six landmark buildings from Kansas City's great Boom Era — City Hall, the Power & Light Building, Municipal Auditorium, and more. The finest concentration of Art Deco architecture between Chicago and Los Angeles.

Start Walking →
Country Club Plaza • 1.0 mile

The Plaza & Fountains Walk

From the J.C. Nichols Memorial Fountain through the Spanish Colonial district to the Fountain of Bacchus. A tour of the sculptural program J.C. Nichols assembled to give his shopping district the character of a European city.

Start Walking →
West Bottoms • 0.8 miles

The West Bottoms & River

The city's industrial origin — the flood plain where Kansas City began as a river trade post, grew into a cattle and rail capital, and left behind a district of massive brick warehouses that still define the skyline from the bluffs above.

Start Walking →
Crossroads Corridor • 1.1 miles

Union Station to Liberty Memorial

Begin in the grand hall of one of America's greatest Beaux Arts train stations, then walk south through the Crossroads to the Liberty Memorial — the only World War I museum and memorial in the country to earn the designation of National Museum.

Start Walking →

Planning Your Visit

All four tours are entirely outdoors and accessible year-round, though Kansas City summers are hot and humid — early morning or late afternoon are the best times to walk between June and August. The Art Deco Mile and the Union Station corridor are downtown routes; the Plaza walk is in the Country Club Plaza district, about four miles south of downtown. The West Bottoms is best visited on a weekend, when the antique dealers and small businesses that have colonized the old warehouse buildings are open.

Each tour page is formatted for printing. Bring a printout and walk without your phone in your hand — you'll look up more.
